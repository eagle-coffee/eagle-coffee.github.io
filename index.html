Our Coffee

Washed Coffee

Also known as Wet Processed Coffee, the coffee cherry is pulped by a machine, removing the red outer skin of the cherry. Once this is done, the seeds with their mucilage are then fermented in water for 1-2 days, or sometimes longer. This often creates a bean that is much cleaner and brighter and tastes much fruitier than the unwashed method. Some of the world’s finest coffees are created through this process.

Natural Coffee

Also known as Unwashed Coffee. In Natural Coffee processing, we place coffee cherries on a flat surface where they are dried in the sun. This process normally takes about 2-6 weeks, and the beans are raked and rotated so that they dry evenly. Once the seeds have dried, they are then removed from the cherry. This method creates a coffee that is heavy in body but remains sweet, smooth, and complex.

Yirgacheffe

Yirgacheffe is one of the best highland-grown coffees. It has fine acidity, body, and flavor. Many people are attracted to its delicate, floral, and tea-like characteristics. Yirgacheffe is a sweet flavor and has an aroma with a light to medium body. Sidama is a wet-processed (washed) Ethiopian coffee known for its rich, full body (mouthfeel), sweet and complex flavor, low acidity, floral aroma, and an finish that is bright and soft.

Sidamo

This type is grown in the Sidama region south of the capital, Addis Ababa. It is the most southern and most productive province in the country. The region is well known for having ideal climate conditions for growing coffee. This is all due to high altitudes of between a 1600 - 1900 meters, ample rainfall, optimal temperature, and rich soil.

Limu

Limu is a region in southwest Ethiopia. This coffee is very similar in cup profile to washed Yirgacheffe coffee because of its full citrus acidity, soft berry undertones, chocolate and floral notes. Limu coffee is grown at about 1,100-1,800 meters altitude

Sidamo

Sidama is a wet-processed (washed) Ethiopian coffee known for its rich, full body (mouthfeel), sweet and complex flavor, low acidity, floral aroma, and a finish that is bright and soft. A good Sidama is well-balanced with notes of chocolate and and often exhibits tones of spice and wine.

Gimbi

Grown in the Ghimbi region of Ethiopia at elevations between 4,900 feet and 5,900 feet above sea level, Gimbi coffee is known for its pleasant acidity and healthy body reminiscent of Ethiopian Harrar Longberry coffee.

Lekempti

Grown in the Wollega region of Ethiopia at elevations between 4,900 feet and 5,900 feet above sea level, Lekempti coffee is known for its pleasant acidity and healthy body reminiscent of Ethiopian Harrar Longberry coffee.

Djimmah

An excellent, low-acid Ethiopian coffee when it is wet-processed (washed). When Djimmah is dry processed (natural; unwashed), however, it is known to impart a generally undesirable medicinal flavor.
